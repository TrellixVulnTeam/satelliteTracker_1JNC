About The Satellite Tracker

Controls:

Click and drag to move the globe around

Press the space bar to start/stop camera rotation around the globe.

Scroll on the satellite or groundsite list to view all selectable options

Click on the checkbox next to a satellite or groundsite name to turn that satellite's path or groundsite location on or off

Scroll with the mouse to zoom in and out

Click on the path or image of a satellite or the to highlight it. Click off the satellite path or image to deselect it.

About:

This project tracks 100 different satellites in their orbits around the earth, with 10 groundsites on the earth's surface. The point of the project is to visualize satellite paths in 3D, while also showing where those paths are visible to different points on the earth.

The application allows for the user to select and view satellite orbits, and the path shown is a 12 hour snapshot of the satellite's future path. A ground site can also be selected to show where a satellite's orbit will be visible to that location on the earth. The project also uses a shader to show day and night on the earth.

Some of the orbits, especially high earth orbits, will look a little odd, and this is expected. This globe doesn't rotate independently beneath the satellites. Because of this, the orbits may look different than expected as they stay accurate to which parts of the earth they are above at given times. It would look more like what we are used to when viewing a 3D orbit had I opted to create an elliptical path for each satellite based on the TLE information gathered. Because the purpose of the project was to use the TLE data for each satellite to show as accurately as possible where each satellite will be in relation to the earth's surface, I opted for what you see now. This was also more interesting to work with.
