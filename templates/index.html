About The Satellite Tracker

Controls:

Click and drag to move the globe around

Press the space bar to start/stop camera rotation around the globe.

Scroll on the satellite or groundsite list to view all selectable options

Click on the checkbox next to a satellite or groundsite name to turn that satellite's path or groundsite location on or off

Scroll with the mouse to zoom in and out

Click on the path of a satellite to highlight it. Click the path again or select another satellite path to deselect it.

About:

This project tracks 100 different satellites in their orbits around the earth, with 10 groundsites on the earth's surface. The point of the project is to visualize satellite paths in 3D, while also showing where those paths are visible to different points on the earth.

The application allows for the user to select and view satellite orbits, and the path shown is a 12 hour snapshot of the satellite's future path. The user can also select a ground site to show where the satellite's orbit will be visible to that location on the earth. The project also uses a light shining on the globe to show which parts of the earth are currently in daylight.

Some of the high earth orbits will look a little crazy, which is expected. This globe doesn't rotate independently beneath the satellites. This means the orbits can start to look odd because the path shows where in relation to the non-rotating earth each satellite will be at a given time. It would look more normal had I opted to create an elliptical path for each satellite based on the TLE information gathered, but I thought this was more interesting.
